# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Visitors arriving at a designer's personal home base: peers in the design/creative scene, potential collaborators, the occasional recruiter or client, and friends following a shared link. They show up curious, on desktop or phone, usually for a short visit. The job to be done is simple but high-stakes: in a few seconds, understand *who this person is*, feel their taste, and remember them. This is an identity card, not a lead funnel, so the primary "action" is an impression rather than a conversion.

## Product Purpose

A one-page personal portfolio that functions as a self-expressive home base for a designer. It exists to project a distinct identity and showcase work, not to optimize a sales pipeline. Success looks like: a visitor immediately gets the vibe, the site feels unmistakably *theirs* (not a template), and it's memorable enough to share. The design itself is the proof of skill, so the execution has to demonstrate the craft it claims.

## Brand Personality

All-in cyberpunk / Edgerunners. Loud, edgy, glitchy, neon-soaked Night City energy. The voice is confident, a little defiant, terminal/operator-flavored (system readouts, `// labels`, status tickers, "operator profile"). Three words: **electric, defiant, cinematic.** Emotional goal: a jolt of adrenaline and intrigue, the sense of stepping into a different decade's idea of the future. Personality is the entire point; distinctiveness beats polish-by-committee every time.

## Anti-references

- **Corporate / LinkedIn-bland.** Safe, beige, forgettable, personality-free. The single thing this site must never become.
- **Generic SaaS/startup template.** Inter font, soft purple gradients, rounded cards, "modern minimal" defaults, generic AI aesthetics.
- **The ubiquitous all-white minimalist designer portfolio.** Lots of whitespace, thin gray type, no point of view.

## Design Principles

- **The world is the message.** Commit fully to the cyberpunk universe. Half-measures read as costume; full immersion reads as identity. When unsure, go further in, not safer.
- **Practice what you preach.** As a designer's site, the build *is* the portfolio piece. Sloppy execution undermines the claim of skill more than any missing project would.
- **Atmosphere is structural, not decorative.** The video, scanlines, grain, glitch, custom cursor, and motion are load-bearing to the identity. They are the product, not garnish to strip when convenient.
- **Memorable over tasteful-by-committee.** A personal-presence site wins by being unforgettable and unmistakably its owner's. Never trade that for generic "best practices" safety.
- **Loud, but never illegible.** Intensity is the brand, but core content (name, what they do, how to reach them) must still land instantly. Spectacle that obscures the message fails its one job.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Not a formal priority for this project: aesthetics intentionally win over strict WCAG conformance, and the heavy animation (glitch, scanlines, grain, looping video) is core to the identity rather than something to gate behind reduced-motion. No target WCAG level is committed. The one non-negotiable baseline is that essential content stays legible: the name, role, project text, and contact links must remain readable against their backgrounds. There is no requirement to add reduced-motion fallbacks unless the owner later changes this stance.
